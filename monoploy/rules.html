Rules

  1. Drive your opponents to bankruptcy for victory
  2. Land on properties to buy them
  3. All three properties of a set must be owned to build houses
  4. Houses must be built evenly
  5. Rolling doubles, or rolling two of the same number, allows you to take another turn
  6. Go to jail if three doubles are thrown in a row
  7. Land on a space marked "Go to Jail" means you have to go to jail
  8. Draw a card marked "Go to Jail" means you have to go to jail
  9. To get out of jail, you may pay 50$ before you roll, you may use "Get Out Of Jail Free Card" before rolling the dice, or roll doubles. You are given three rolls of the dice beofre you have to pay $50
  10. Pay rent of a propertie if owned by an opponent

Tips

  1. If in debt, properties can be mortgaged for half of what it is worth
  2. Mortgaged properties can be unmortgaged for full price
  3. Houses can be sold for half of what the house is worth
  4. The more railroads owned the more money is made from rent
  5. Higher value properties take more money to build houses on, better to build on lower value properties
  6. If you are having trouble getting money to build houses, mortgage all the properties not part of a completed set

Turn Actions

  1. Roll the dice. Move the number of squares indicated. If you throw doubles, you take another turn after your turn is completed. Each time you pass "Go", collect $200 from the Bank.
  2. Buy properties. You may buy any property from the Bank that you land on if it is not already owned. If you do not purchase the property, the Banker auctions the property to the highest bidder.
  3. Building. You may only build when you own all properties in a color group. Building must be equal on all properties in a group. You may place a single building on a single property, but you may not place two buildings on one property unless all other properties in the group have one building present (even build rule). Any property can have a total of 4 houses, except Utilities and Railroads, which cannot be devloped. To place a hotel on a property, 4 houses must be present on all properties in the group. Houses are removed from the property when a hotel is placed. All buildings are purchased from the Bank.
  4. Complete necessary actions. Pay rent as determined by the Title Deed for the property you are on. Pay Income Tax to the Bank ($200 or 10% of your total assets). Draw a Community Chest or Chance card and follow the instructions. These cards are returned to the bottom of the pile when the action is completed.

Classic Rules

Regular rules of Monopoly, no difference

Whenever a player is taxed or loses money due to a card that money is stored in the free parking space. If a player lands on the free parking space then collect all the money in that space

If a player is in jail, the other players may land on their owned property and not have to pay them

When a player lands on a unowned property, they must auction it. Every player in the game participates in the auction
